About This Project

This blog serves as our submission for the Year's Work Project in the Novel EN-PRNV22 course. Through two comprehensive entries, we explore the complex representations of imperialism in 18th-century English literature, focusing on Daniel Defoe's Robinson Crusoe and Jonathan Swift's Gulliver's Travels.

Our project examines how these seminal works reflect, critique, and interrogate the imperialistic practices of their time, offering insights into colonial ideology, cultural encounters, and the construction of the "civilized" versus the "savage."
